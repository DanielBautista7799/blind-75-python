## Problem Summary

Given a list of heights, find the two lines that can hold the most water between them.

The amount of water depends on the shorter height and the width between the two indexes.

## Inputs and Expected Output

- Input: a list of integers representing heights.
- Output: the maximum possible area.

## Edge Cases to Consider

- Only two heights.
- Heights with equal values.
- Strictly increasing heights.
- Strictly decreasing heights.
- Tallest height does not always create the largest area.
- Maximum area is found between far apart indexes.
